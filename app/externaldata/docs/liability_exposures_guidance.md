# Liability Exposures Guidance (Synthetic)
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Version:** v0.1-synthetic

## 1. Purpose
This document defines how personal-liability exposures on a homeowners risk map to underwriting actions. It is synthetic and complements the eligibility, hazard, and endorsement manuals.

## 2. Animal Liability
### 2.1 Dog Breed Considerations
- A documented bite history within the prior five years **SHALL** refer to an underwriter regardless of breed.
- Properties with three or more dogs **SHOULD** prompt a liability questionnaire.
- The carrier does not maintain a banned-breed list in this demo; decisions **MUST** rest on documented behavior, not breed alone.

### 2.2 Exotic and Farm Animals
- Exotic animals (non-domesticated) kept on the premises **MUST** refer.
- Hobby-farm livestock **MAY** require a separate farm or excess liability form.

## 3. Attractive Nuisances
### 3.1 Swimming Pools and Spas
- An in-ground or above-ground pool **SHOULD** trigger a fencing-and-gate confirmation.
- Pools without a compliant barrier (self-latching gate, minimum height) **SHALL** refer.
- Diving boards and pool slides **MAY** warrant a higher liability limit or exclusion.

### 3.2 Trampolines and Play Structures
- A trampoline **SHALL** refer unless safety netting and anchoring are confirmed.
- Treehouses and zip lines **SHOULD** be noted for the underwriter.

## 4. Home-Based Business Liability
- Incidental home business with client foot traffic **SHOULD** recommend an endorsement or separate commercial policy.
- Childcare or food-service operations on premises **MUST** refer.

## 5. Liability Limit Guidance
- Base Coverage E (personal liability) below the program minimum **SHALL** require a limit increase before binding.
- Risks with multiple attractive nuisances **SHOULD** recommend an umbrella referral.
- Documented prior liability losses **MUST** be summarized in the decision packet for the underwriter.
