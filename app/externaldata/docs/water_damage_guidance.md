# Water Damage Guidance (Synthetic)
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Version:** v0.1-synthetic

## 1. Purpose
This document covers non-flood water exposures and the mitigation evidence that supports a favorable decision. Flood (SFHA) handling lives in the hazard guidance; this manual addresses interior and seepage water loss.

## 2. Water Backup and Sump Failure
### 2.1 Sump Pump Risks
- Finished basements without a working sump pump **SHOULD** prompt a water backup question.
- A sump pump with no battery backup in a high-water-table area **MAY** recommend a backup-of-sewer-and-drain endorsement.

### 2.2 Backup Endorsement Eligibility
- A prior sewer or drain backup loss **SHALL** refer for endorsement pricing.
- Two or more backup losses in five years **MUST** refer with loss detail attached.

## 3. Freeze and Seepage
### 3.1 Seasonal and Vacant Exposure
- Seasonally vacant homes in freeze-prone territories **SHOULD** confirm winterization or a low-temperature monitor.
- Continuous seepage or standing water near the foundation **SHALL** refer for a moisture inspection.

### 3.2 Leak Detection Credits
- A whole-home automatic leak-shutoff device **MAY** earn a mitigation credit where the rating plan supports it.
- Smart water sensors alone **SHOULD** be noted but do not change eligibility.

## 4. Appliance and Supply-Line Losses
- A history of repeated supply-line or water-heater failures **SHALL** refer.
- Polybutylene supply lines **MUST** be flagged for the underwriter and **SHOULD** recommend replacement.

## 5. Documentation for Favorable Decisions
- Photo evidence of mitigation devices **SHOULD** be cited in the decision packet.
- A signed maintenance attestation **MAY** offset a single prior water loss at underwriter discretion.
